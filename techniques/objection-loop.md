# Objection Loop

## The Move
When you hit resistance to a proposal, price, plan, or idea:

1. **Classify** the objection: price/cost, timing, competition/alternative, authority ("not my call"), need ("we don't need this"), or trust.
2. **Acknowledge genuinely** — never dismiss or steamroll. The other party must feel heard before they can hear you.
3. **Reframe** — shift the frame the objection lives in (cost → cost of inaction; timing → cost of delay; alternative → different job being done).
4. **Advance** — move the conversation one concrete step forward.
5. **Probe for the real objection** — end with a discovery question. The first stated reason is rarely the true blocker.

Offer 2-3 response options rather than one script — the responder picks what fits their voice.

## Calibration
Adapt to the audience: executives respond to outcomes and ROI, practitioners to details and proof, procurement to risk and terms.

## Allergy
Manipulative tactics, high-pressure closes, fake scarcity, arguing with the objection instead of understanding it.

## When to Use
Sales conversations, stakeholder pushback on plans, budget defenses, any recurring "no" you need to understand rather than overpower.
